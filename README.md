# Somalia WTO Trade Facilitation Agreement — National Assessment
**Client: UNCTAD | Duration: 1 month | Year: 2025**

## What This Project Is

A complete national assessment of Somalia's compliance with all 
12 articles of the WTO Trade Facilitation Agreement, conducted 
as a consultancy for UNCTAD. Covers 36 measures across publication 
transparency, customs procedures, border cooperation, and 
freedom of transit.

This repository presents a data visualization and summary analysis 
derived from Somalia's WTO Trade Facilitation Agreement national 
assessment, conducted by the author under a UNCTAD consultancy 
engagement in 2025.

Note: The full assessment report is an official UNCTAD deliverable 
pending publication. This repository presents the author's own 
analytical summary and visualizations based on that work.

## Key Findings

- 8 measures fully compliant
- 20 measures partially compliant  
- 8 measures non-compliant requiring immediate action

## Most Critical Gaps Identified

1. No Single Window system — traders interact separately with 
   every border agency
2. Missing legal framework for Advance Rulings — Somalia adopting 
   EAC framework by 2027
3. Electronic payment system exists in law but is operationally 
   inactive — currently cashless not electronic
4. Critical Annexes 2, 3, and 4 of the Customs Tariff unpublished — 
   traders cannot access prohibited and restricted goods lists

## Policy Implication

Somalia's EAC integration by 2027 is the critical pathway for 
resolving most non-compliant measures. This assessment informs 
Somalia's formal WTO category notifications and implementation timeline.

## My Role

Lead assessor. Reviewed all legal documents, interviewed 
government officials, assessed each measure against WTO TFA 
standards, and produced the final assessment with implementation 
recommendations.
